# TakeMeter — Label Taxonomy

**Community:** r/QuantumComputing

## Labels

### `news`
Shares an external announcement, paper, or industry development — the post's value comes from the linked content, not the poster's argument. May include a brief reaction but no original reasoning is built.

> *Example:* "Microsoft's Majorana 2 Topological Quantum Computer"
> *Example:* "QpiAI Achieves High-Speed Quantum Error Correction on Superconducting Systems"

### `discussion`
The poster has a perspective, observation, or claim they want the community to debate. No single correct answer exists — the post is valuable because it invites expert opinions or analysis.

> *Example:* "When will SC qubits start to die off?"
> *Example:* "What do you think of QuEra's 'Fault-tolerance in 2028' — is it a bold claim?"

### `question`
The poster lacks understanding of something and wants an explanation. A single well-informed person could answer it correctly by citing a source, paper, or established concept.

> *Example:* "I don't get generalized amplitude damping"
> *Example:* "Why don't we just perform another transform in the Fourier basis after QFT?"

## Hard Edge Case

**Post:** *"What do you think actually counts as a quantum measurement?"*

Could be `question` (grammatically) or `discussion` (no textbook answer, invites philosophical debate).

**Decision rule:** If the post could be answered correctly by one person citing a source → `question`. If it requires community debate and has no single correct answer → `discussion`. The measurement post has no settled answer → `discussion`.
